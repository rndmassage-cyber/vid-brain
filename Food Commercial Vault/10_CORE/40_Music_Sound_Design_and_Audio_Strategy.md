# Music, Sound Design and Audio Strategy

## Why This Matters
Sound modifies taste perception. High-pitched audio makes food taste sweeter; low-pitched makes it more bitter (Spence, 2008). A sizzle alone triggers salivation via Pavlovian response. Sound-on viewers convert at significantly higher rates. Audio is not decoration — it is a craving lever.

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Identify the dish's **signature sound** (sizzle, crunch, pour, fizz) before choosing music. That sound leads the mix.
- Mix hierarchy: **(1) hero food sound → (2) supporting foley → (3) music bed.** Never reverse.
- Target **-14 LUFS integrated** for all social platforms. Test on phone speakers.
- Design for **mute-first** (visual rhythm + captions), reward **sound-on** (foley + music).
- Use **0.5–1 sec of near-silence before the hero sound** — anticipation dopamine is stronger than reward dopamine.
- **Congruence > novelty.** Save unexpected sonic pairings for the brand sting, not the food moment.

## Commercial Style by Audio Profile

| Commercial Style | Lead Audio | Music Role | Energy |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Premium cinematic | Minimal / none during food | Emotional underscore (piano, strings) | Low–medium |
| Beat-sync / social | Music-first | Drives edit rhythm | Medium–high |
| ASMR | Food foley dominant | Absent or near-absent | Intimate |
| UGC / authentic | Casual music or ambient | Background only | Variable |
| Process / recipe | Hybrid foley + lo-fi bed | Supports but does not dominate | Medium |
| Fast casual / QSR | Upbeat, rhythmic | Reinforces energy and service pace | High |

## Tempo Rules by Food Category

| Food Type | BPM Range | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Fine dining / luxury | 60–80 | Slow tempo extends savoring; signals quality |
| Comfort food / indulgent | 85–95 | Sweet spot for maximum appetite response |
| Casual / everyday | 90–110 | Comfortable energy, relatable |
| Street food / fast casual | 100–120 | Fast tempo raises arousal and purchase intent |
| Desserts / pastry / baking | 70–90 | Calm, cozy pacing |
| Cocktails / drinks | 85–105 | Smooth, aspirational |

**Cut math:** At 100 BPM = 0.6s per beat. At 120 BPM = 2 beats/sec. Calculate frames-per-beat when placing markers.

## Frequency–Taste Correspondence

Experimentally validated crossmodal mappings (Crisinel & Spence, 2010; Motoki et al., 2023):

| Taste / Texture | Frequency | Instruments | Character |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Sweet | High (>1 kHz) | Piano, flute, harp | Consonant, legato |
| Sour | High, staccato | High woodwinds | Fast, bright |
| Bitter | Low (<500 Hz) | Brass, cello | Minor, sustained |
| Umami / rich | Low-mid | Low brass, warm pads | Deep, sustained |
| Crispy / fresh | >5 kHz | Bells, pizzicato | Staccato, sharp |
| Creamy / smooth | Broad low-mid | Legato strings, pads | Smooth, no transients |

**Production rule:** Boost 5–12 kHz for crispness perception. Boost 100–300 Hz for richness. Cut 2–4 kHz to reduce harshness.

## Cultural Music Congruence

Ethnically matched music primes food choice at high unconscious ratios (North et al., 1999). This is a measured behavioral effect, not a stylistic preference.

| Cuisine | Musical Coding | BPM |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Italian | Romantic vocal, mandolin, classical guitar | 70–90 |
| Japanese | Koto, shakuhachi, minimal piano | Sparse, pentatonic |
| French fine dining | Classical, jazz standards, piano trio | 60–80 |
| American BBQ / comfort | Blues, country, Americana | 90–110 |
| Asian street food | Lo-fi beats with traditional instruments | 100–120 |
| Ethiopian / Middle Eastern | Oud, frame drum, modal scales | Modal, rhythmic |
| Desserts / chocolate | Smooth jazz, piano, high-pitched melodies | 70–90 |

## When to Use Music vs Foley vs Hybrid

| Scenario | Lead with |
|----------|-----------|
| Dish has a signature sound (sizzle, crunch, fizz, pour) | **Foley** — the sound IS the hook |
| Visually beautiful but sonically quiet (plated dessert, sushi) | **Music** — create mood where sound cannot |
| Process/recipe video | **Hybrid** — lo-fi bed under prominent foley |
| Brand awareness / emotional campaign | **Music** — emotional arc carries |
| TikTok/Reels viral attempt | **ASMR foley** or trending audio |
| Luxury / fine dining | **Music** (minimal piano/jazz) or strategic silence |

## The Audio Arc (15 seconds)

Map to the vault's 4-phase visual structure:

| Phase | Time | Audio Role | Technique |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Hook** | 0–3s | Signature food sound or arresting silence | Lead with foley or start sparse; music enters low |
| **Crave / Reveal** | 3–7s | Music builds; food foley continues | Rising pitch, unresolved chord, increasing density |
| **Interaction** | 7–12s | **Peak** — hero food sound at maximum presence | 0.5s silence before hero sound; music ducks -6 dB |
| **Payoff** | 12–15s | Resolution — music resolves; sound settles | Consonant chord, legato; brand moment rides peak |

**Critical:** The silence-before-the-sizzle is not optional. Jumping straight to the hero sound wastes the most powerful mechanism available in 15 seconds.

## Hero Food Sound Catalog

### Tier 1 — Primary Appetite Triggers
| Sound | Best For | Note |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Sizzle** | Meat, stir-fry, grilled | Universal Pavlovian trigger |
| **Crunch / snap** | Fried, fresh veg, chips, crust | Louder + higher pitched = fresher perception |
| **Pour** | Sauces, drinks, glazing | Slower pour = thicker perceived |
| **Fizz** | Beer, soda, sparkling | High-frequency trigger |
| **Knife on board** | Prep sequences, precision | Wooden board > plastic (richer tone) |

### Tier 2 — Supporting Sounds
| Sound | Best For |
|-------|----------|
| Salt crystal crush | Finishing, fine dining |
| Bread tear / crust crack | Bakery, artisan |
| Cheese pull / stretch | Pizza, melt shots |
| Boiling / bubbling | Soups, stews, sauces |
| Steam release | Coffee, ramen, fresh baked |
| Drip (delayed) | Thick sauce proof — delayed drip = thick |

## Sound Layer Architecture

Professional standard: **3–5 layers maximum** per composite food sound.

| Layer | Content | Level |
|-------|---------|-------|
| 1. **Hero sound** | Primary food sound | Loudest; drives the mix |
| 2. **Texture / detail** | Secondary texture filling frequency gaps | -3 to -6 dB below hero |
| 3. **Environment** | Room tone, kitchen ambience | Bed level; felt not heard |
| 4. **Sweetener** | EQ'd/processed enhancement of hero | Subtle blend; barely perceptible |
| 5. **Music bed** | Underscore | Must not mask food sounds |

---

## Silence as a Tool

- **0.5–1 sec silence before the hero sound** increases retention vs continuous music.
- Strategic silence before the money shot creates the strongest anticipation spike available in short-form.
- **Never use dead silence** — always maintain subtle room tone to prevent "vacuum" feeling.
- After a peak event, allow room tone to re-establish for 0.3–0.5 sec before the next event.

---

## Mixing Rules

| Rule | Value |
|------|-------|
| Music under hero foley | Duck -6 to -9 dB during food sounds |
| Overall target | -14 LUFS integrated |
| True peak ceiling | -1 dBTP |
| Stereo | Collapse to mono-compatible; most phone speakers are mono |
| Low-end | High-pass below 80–100 Hz (phones can't reproduce it) |
| Presence boost | +2 to +4 dB at 2–5 kHz for clarity on phone speakers |

---

## Decision Rules — Ask Before Designing Audio

1. What is the dish's signature sound? (sizzle, crunch, pour, fizz — pick the lead)
2. Should this ad lead with foley, music, or silence at the 0-second mark?
3. What tempo matches this food's emotional category? (use BPM table)
4. Where is the "money shot" — the single peak audio moment and its timecode?
5. What cultural music coding matches this cuisine? (use congruence table)
6. Will 85% of viewers see this muted — does the visual arc work alone?
7. What frequency range should dominate — crispy high (>5 kHz) or rich low (<500 Hz)?
8. Is this a reach campaign (trending audio) or brand/hero campaign (original foley/music)?
9. What platform is primary — determines delivery spec and strategy?
10. Is ASMR appropriate — broad audience (no) or food-native community (yes)?
