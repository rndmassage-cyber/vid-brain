# Music, Sound Design and Audio Strategy

## Why This Matters
Sound modifies taste perception. High-pitched audio makes food taste 10% sweeter; low-pitched makes it 10% more bitter (Spence, Oxford, 2008). A sizzle alone triggers salivation — Pavlovian response without any visual. 85% of social video is watched muted, but sound-on viewers convert at significantly higher rates. Audio is not decoration — it is a craving lever.

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Identify the dish's **signature sound** (sizzle, crunch, pour, fizz) before choosing music. That sound leads the mix.
- Mix hierarchy: **(1) hero food sound → (2) supporting foley → (3) music bed.** Never reverse.
- Target **-14 LUFS integrated** for all social platforms. Test on phone speakers.
- Design for **mute-first** (visual rhythm + captions), reward **sound-on** (foley + music).
- Use **0.5–1 sec of near-silence before the hero sound** — anticipation dopamine is stronger than reward dopamine (Salimpoor, Nature Neuroscience, 2011).

## Tempo Rules by Food Category

| Food Type | BPM | Why |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Fine dining / luxury | 60–80 | Slow tempo extends savoring; signals quality (Caldwell & Hibbert, 2002) |
| Comfort food / indulgent | 85–95 | Sweet spot for maximum appetite (Sato et al., 2023) |
| Casual / everyday | 90–110 | Comfortable energy, relatable |
| Street food / fast casual | 100–120 | Fast tempo raises arousal and purchase intent (JRCS, 2021) |
| Desserts / pastry / baking | 70–90 | Calm, cozy pacing |
| Cocktails / drinks | 85–105 | Smooth, aspirational |

## Frequency–Taste Correspondence

Experimentally validated crossmodal mappings (Crisinel & Spence, 2010):

| Taste/Texture | Frequency | Instruments | Musical Character |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Sweet | High pitch (>1 kHz) | Piano, flute, harp, high strings | Consonant, legato |
| Sour | High pitch, staccato | High woodwinds | Fast, bright, accented |
| Bitter | Low pitch (<500 Hz) | Brass, cello, low strings | Minor key, sustained |
| Umami / rich | Low-mid pitch | Low brass, warm pads | Sustained, deep |
| Crispy / fresh | >5 kHz | Bells, pizzicato, bright percussion | Staccato, sharp attack |
| Creamy / smooth | Broad low-mid | Legato strings, pads | Smooth, no transients |

**Production rule:** Boost 5–12 kHz in mix for crispness perception. Boost 100–300 Hz for richness. Cut 2–4 kHz to reduce harshness. Roll off below 80 Hz unless the dish needs heaviness.

## Cultural Music Congruence

Ethnically matched music primes food choice at 5:1 ratios unconsciously (North et al., 1999). This is not creative preference — it is a measured behavioural effect.

| Cuisine | Musical Coding | Key |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Italian | Romantic vocal, mandolin, classical guitar | Warm, legato, 70–90 BPM |
| Japanese | Koto, shakuhachi, minimal piano | Sparse, pentatonic, serene |
| French fine dining | Classical, jazz standards, piano trio | Sophisticated, consonant, 60–80 BPM |
| American BBQ / comfort | Blues, country, Americana | Warm, rhythmic, 90–110 BPM |
| Asian street food | Lo-fi beats with traditional instruments | Urban texture, 100–120 BPM |
| Ethiopian / Middle Eastern | Oud, frame drum, modal scales | Modal, warm, rhythmic pulse |
| Desserts / chocolate | Smooth jazz, piano, high-pitched melodies | Sweet-enhancing: high pitch, legato |

**Rule:** Congruence > novelty. Save unexpected pairings for the brand sting, not the food moment.

## When to Use Music vs Foley vs Hybrid

| Scenario | Lead with |
|----------|-----------|
| Dish has a signature sound (sizzle, crunch, fizz, pour) | **Foley** — the sound IS the hook |
| Visually beautiful but sonically quiet (plated dessert, sushi) | **Music** — create mood where sound cannot |
| Process/recipe video | **Hybrid** — lo-fi bed under prominent foley |
| Brand awareness / emotional campaign | **Music** — emotional arc carries |
| TikTok/Reels viral attempt | **ASMR foley** or trending audio |
| Luxury / fine dining | **Music** (minimal piano/jazz) or strategic silence |

## The Audio Arc (15 seconds)

Map to the vault's 4-phase visual structure:

| Phase | Time | Audio Role | Technique |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Hook** | 0–3s | Signature food sound or arresting silence | Lead with foley or start sparse; music enters low |
| **Crave/Reveal** | 3–7s | Music builds; food foley continues | Rising pitch, unresolved chord, increasing density |
| **Interaction** | 7–12s | **Peak** — hero food sound at maximum presence | 0.5s silence before hero sound; music ducks -6 dB; foley forward |
| **Payoff** | 12–15s | Resolution — music resolves; sound settles | Consonant chord, legato, warm; brand moment rides the emotional peak |

**Critical:** The silence-before-the-sizzle is not optional. Anticipation (caudate dopamine) requires contrast. Jumping straight to the hero sound wastes the most powerful neurological mechanism.

## Hero Food Sound Catalog

### Tier 1 — Primary Appetite Triggers
| Sound | Best For | Note |
|-------|----------|------|
| **Sizzle** | Meat, stir-fry, grilled | Universal; Pavlovian conditioned stimulus |
| **Crunch / snap** | Fried, fresh veg, chips, crust | Louder + higher pitched = 15% fresher perception (Spence, 2004) |
| **Pour** | Sauces, drinks, glazing | Signals abundance; slower pour = thicker perceived |
| **Fizz** | Beer, soda, sparkling | Coca-Cola built an empire on pour+fizz+clink |
| **Knife on board** | Prep sequences, precision | Wooden board >> plastic (richer tone) |

### Tier 2 — Supporting Sounds
| Sound | Best For |
|-------|----------|
| Salt crystal crush | Finishing, fine dining |
| Bread tear / crust crack | Bakery, artisan |
| Cheese pull / stretch | Pizza, melt shots |
| Boiling / bubbling | Soups, stews, sauces |
| Steam release | Coffee, ramen, fresh baked |
| Egg crack | Breakfast, baking |
| Drip (delayed) | Thick sauce proof — delayed drip = thick (vault 18) |

## Sound Layer Architecture

Professional standard: **3–5 layers maximum** per composite food sound.

| Layer | Content | Level |
|-------|---------|-------|
| 1. **Hero sound** | Primary food sound | Loudest; drives the mix |
| 2. **Texture/detail** | Secondary texture filling frequency gaps | -3 to -6 dB below hero |
| 3. **Environment** | Room tone, kitchen ambience | Bed level; felt not heard |
| 4. **Sweetener** | EQ'd/processed enhancement of hero | Subtle blend; barely perceptible |
| 5. **Music bed** | Underscore | Must not mask food sounds |

**Frequency splitting:** Each layer occupies a different band. Sub (<200 Hz): boil, impact. Low-mid (200–600 Hz): sizzle body, pour thickness. Mid (600 Hz–2 kHz): knife work, utensil. Presence (2–4 kHz): crispness. Air (4–20 kHz): fizz, steam, high crunch.

## Mixing Rules

| Rule | Value |
|------|-------|
| Music under hero foley | Duck -6 to -9 dB during food sounds |
| Overall target | -14 LUFS integrated (all social platforms) |
| True peak ceiling | -1 dBTP |
| Background noise threshold | Keep under 70 dB — above this, sweetness and salt perception degrade (Woods, 2011) |
| Stereo | Collapse to mono-compatible; most phone speakers are mono |
| Low-end | High-pass everything below 80–100 Hz that doesn't need sub-bass (phones can't reproduce it) |
| Presence boost | +2 to +4 dB at 2–5 kHz for clarity on phone speakers |

## Platform Strategy

| Platform | Sound Behaviour | Strategy |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| Instagram Reels | 85% watched muted; feed defaults sound-on | Mute-first design; reward sound-on; trending audio boosts reach |
| TikTok | 88% say sound is essential; algorithm distributes by sound | Original ASMR foley or trending sounds; early adoption > late |
| YouTube Shorts | Music + narration = 17% better completion | Blend trending audio with original; music should not overpower |

**All platforms normalize to -14 LUFS.** Uploading hotter triggers aggressive limiting that crushes dynamics.

## ASMR Strategy

ASMR works when:
- The dish has rich audible textures (sizzle, crunch, pour, bubble)
- The camera is close (macro, ECU)
- The ad is intimate and focused
- The target audience is food-engaged (not broad brand awareness)

ASMR risks:
- Polarizing — not everyone responds positively
- Requires high-quality recording (cheap recording = cheap perception)
- Pure food foley offers the same benefit with fewer drawbacks for broad audiences

**Rule:** For broad audiences, use prominent food foley with a light music bed rather than pure ASMR. For food-native platforms (TikTok food, food Instagram), pure ASMR outperforms.

## Silence as a Tool

- 0.5–1 sec silence before the hero sound increases retention vs continuous music (Olsen, 1995).
- ASMR-style food ads work because everything is reduced to intimate food sounds in near-silence.
- Strategic silence before the "money shot" creates the anticipation spike.
- **Never use dead silence** — always have a subtle room tone or low ambient to prevent the "vacuum" feeling.

## What Makes It Sound Expensive
- Controlled hero sound at the right moment
- Music that matches the food's emotional temperature
- Clear frequency separation between layers
- Strategic silence before impact
- Food sounds that match the visual physics
- Mix tested on phone speakers
- Culturally congruent music choice

## What Makes It Sound Cheap
- Music louder than food sounds
- Flat front-loaded audio with no arc
- Generic stock music with no cultural or emotional match
- Food sounds mismatched to visual (wet squish for something crispy)
- Over-compressed, no dynamics
- Too many simultaneous layers creating mud
- Sound perspective mismatched to camera (distant audio on close-up shot)

## Decision Rules
Ask before designing audio:
1. What is the dish's signature sound?
2. Should this ad lead with foley, music, or silence?
3. What tempo matches this food's emotional category?
4. Where is the "money shot" — the single peak audio moment?
5. What cultural music coding matches this cuisine?
6. Will 85% of viewers see this muted — does the visual arc work alone?
7. What frequency range should dominate — crispy high or rich low?
