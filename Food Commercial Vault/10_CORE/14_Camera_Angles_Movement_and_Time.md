# Camera Angles, Movement and Time

## Why This Matters
Camera design determines whether the food feels monumental, intimate, luxurious, chaotic, or ordinary.

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Choose the angle based on the dish form, not habit.
- Do not move the camera just to prove movement exists.
- Use close scale when texture is the sales point.
- Use wider scale when the viewer must understand dish identity.
- Slow motion is most valuable at the exact point of tactile payoff.
- Repetition of the same move reduces perceived production value.

## Angle Logic by Food Form
### Low / slight-upward angle
Best for:
- burgers
- sandwiches
- layered desserts
- tall pancakes
- anything architectural

Why:
- increases monumentality
- makes layers feel thick and premium
- strengthens appetite through scale

### 45-degree diner angle
Best for:
- plated meals
- soups
- pasta
- steaks
- bowls
- most restaurant-serving views

Why:
- feels human
- balances access and form
- shows both volume and surface

### Top-down / overhead
Best for:
- pizza
- prep boards
- ingredient layouts
- geometric plating
- assembly sequences

Why:
- favors pattern and readability
- reduces ambiguity in layout
- works well for vertical framing if the composition stays bold

## Macro vs Medium vs Hero
### Macro
Use for:
- blistering
- droplets
- steam edge
- crust fracture
- cheese strands
- knife entry
- bubbling fat
- sauce cling

### Medium
Use for:
- readable action
- hand interaction
- ingredient placement
- revealing how the part belongs to the whole

### Hero
Use for:
- final recognition
- emotional reward
- product memory
- clear sales image

Premium ads usually alternate between macro seduction and readable confirmation.

## Push-In, Orbit, Track and Reveal
### Push-in
Use when desire should intensify toward a key texture or action.

### Orbit
Use when:
- the food has dimensional form
- gloss should roll across the surface
- the environment should feel rich but secondary

### Tracking move
Use when:
- following a falling or moving ingredient
- matching motion of a lifted utensil
- keeping a dynamic object centered while background streaks or shifts

### Reveal move
Use when:
- the food should be discovered from behind steam, garnish, packaging, or props
- the payoff benefits from unveiling rather than immediate exposure

## Time Manipulation
### Real-time
Best for:
- confident serving moments
- final plate placement
- clean restaurant gestures
- restrained lifestyle beats

### Slow motion
Best for:
- pours
- pulls
- compression
- tosses
- fracture
- steam-heavy movement

### Selective extreme slow motion
Use only for the most valuable physical event:
- cheese stretch
- splash crown
- sugar or salt burst
- crisp debris flying
- micro-droplets from citrus or glaze

## Speed Ramps
A good speed ramp:
- accelerates attention
- lands on the strongest tactile instant
- returns control before the shot overstays

A bad speed ramp feels like an editing gimmick with no appetite function.

## Scale and Monumentality
Food looks expensive when scale is controlled:
- macro can make ordinary texture feel epic
- low angles enlarge structure
- shallow depth and selective focus create cinematic hierarchy
- moving close to the food increases immediacy

## When to Move the Camera
Move when the movement reveals one of:
- new texture
- new geometry
- appetite access
- environmental context
- a stronger emotional beat

If the shot already has enough action inside the frame, camera movement may be unnecessary.

## When to Keep It Still
Keep the camera stable when:
- the food movement itself is the spectacle
- the action is intricate and must read clearly
- the plate composition is the payoff
- the viewer needs a moment of visual certainty

## What Makes It Feel Premium
- angle chosen for dish physics
- movement tied to a clear reveal or action
- variation in scale
- timing matched to the key edible moment
- elegant restraint between high-energy beats

## What Makes It Feel Random
- every shot floating the same way
- unnecessary orbit on flat food
- macro shots with no identifiable texture target
- speed changes unrelated to appetite peaks
- push-ins that end on the wrong detail
