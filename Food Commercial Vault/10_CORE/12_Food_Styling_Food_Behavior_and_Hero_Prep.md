# Food Styling, Food Behavior and Hero Prep

## Why This Matters
Food changes quickly on camera.  
It can wilt, dry out, separate, collapse, lose steam, absorb liquid, or darken.  
A premium ad treats the dish as a designed on-camera object, not as an uncontrolled real-time meal.

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Style the food for the **camera view**, not for how it would look from every angle.
- Protect the hero textures first.
- Keep the dish readable at a glance.
- Avoid over-cleaning the food until it looks synthetic.
- Use professional handling cues when hands or tools appear.
- Treat garnish as part of the identity, not random decoration.

## How Food Fails on Camera
Typical failure modes:
- cheese goes greasy and shapeless
- sauces run too far and hide structure
- steam disappears
- greens wilt
- fried coatings soften
- bread dries out
- cold drinks lose clean condensation logic
- soups swallow hero ingredients

The solution in this vault is not to replicate physical studio tricks.  
Instead, describe the **visual outcome**:
- slow, heavy sauce flow
- persistent dew-like droplets
- proud hero ingredients above the liquid line
- elastic but controlled stretch
- crisp edges that remain readable

## Controlled Freshness
Freshness should look curated, not chaotic.
Use:
- hydrated surfaces
- crisp-cut edges
- clean herbs
- fresh garnish with purpose
- subtle droplets where appropriate
- lightly revived shine on moist components

Avoid:
- wetting every surface the same way
- drowning the dish in shine
- hyper-symmetry that feels machine-made

## Controlled Imperfection
Perfect food often looks fake.
Good imperfection includes:
- a slightly uneven bun dome
- one meaningful sauce smear
- a few real crumbs
- asymmetrical blistering on crust
- a small natural offset in stacked layers
- a garnish that feels placed, not stamped

Bad imperfection includes:
- messy collapse
- random spills with no design logic
- distracting debris
- too many competing rough textures

## Hero Ingredient Prioritization
Every dish has 1–3 hero elements.
Examples:
- burger: patty, cheese edge, bun compression
- pizza: crust blistering, cheese tension, sauce boundary
- soup: steam, surface richness, spoon lift
- sushi: fish translucency, rice grain definition, clean geometry

The hero elements must remain visible in most shots.

## Hands, Tools, and Handling
When a person interacts with food:
- hands must look intentional and clean
- grip pressure should match the food texture
- tools should look appropriate and professional
- black nitrile gloves are a strong premium cue for prep shots
- chef tweezers, chopsticks, spoons, pizza wheels, or knives should not appear generic if they are the center of attention

## Texture Enhancement Logic
For generative work, describe the visual behavior, not the unsafe real-world material substitute.
Useful visual targets:
- thick syrup-like flow
- stable, spherical condensation
- opaque elastic cheese strands
- ingredients held high in a broth instead of sinking
- thick glaze that coats peaks but does not erase texture

## Camera-Angle-Driven Styling
Style for the chosen angle:
- low angles need clean stacking and underside logic
- 45° angles need appetite access and top-surface readability
- overhead shots need geometry, spacing, and pattern logic
- macro shots need micro-texture priority over total dish visibility

## What Makes Styling Look Premium
- clear hierarchy
- visible hero textures
- tasteful imperfection
- disciplined garnish
- edible access
- precise but not sterile composition
- realistic handling cues

## What Makes Styling Look Fake or Cheap
- every edge perfectly symmetrical
- garnish scattered without purpose
- overfilled plateware hiding the hero food
- identical shine on every surface
- sloppy hands or wrong tools
- props fighting the dish for attention

## Continuity Risks
- garnish count changes between cuts
- the top layer of a stack moves unpredictably
- condensation disappears and returns randomly
- the food is prepped one way, but appears served another way
- a tool changes size, color, or material across shots

## Practical Notes
- Always determine the hero angle before over-describing plating.
- If the ad includes prep, show only prep actions that strengthen appetite.
- For delicate foods, treat structure and freshness as continuity variables.
