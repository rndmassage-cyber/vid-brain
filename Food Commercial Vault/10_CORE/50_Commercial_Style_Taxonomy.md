# Commercial Style Taxonomy

## How to Use

Each style is a complete production lens — philosophy + structure + camera + audio + food fit.
Pick one primary and at most one secondary. Do not blend more than two styles in 15 seconds.

For lighting and color specifics → [[13_Lighting_Gloss_Steam_and_Color]].
For camera movement details → [[14_Camera_Angles_Movement_and_Time]].

## STYLE 01 — Premium Cinematic / Hero Beauty

**Philosophy:** The dish is a monument. Every frame sells premium quality and desire.
**Emotion:** Reverence, aspiration, craving.

**Shot structure (15s, 4–6 shots):**
1. Macro hook — impossibly close texture or gloss (2–3s)
2. Reveal — pull back or orbit shows full dish (3–4s)
3. Interaction — one satisfying action: pour, pull, slice (3–4s)
4. Hero payoff — composed full dish at ideal angle (3–4s)

**Camera:** Low-to-mid angle; slow push-ins and orbits; probe lens for texture; shallow DOF throughout. Slow motion at 120fps for pour/pull moments only.
**Audio:** Hero food sound leads mix. Music 60–95 BPM, minimal. 0.5–1s silence before peak sound.
**Best foods:** Burgers, steaks, pasta with sauce, pizza, desserts with layers, sushi, noodle bowls.
**Avoid when:** Foods lack premium cues (plain boiled items), no dimensional form for orbits.
**Continuity:** Lock sauce placement, garnish count, plateware, stack layer order. Interaction aftermath must persist.

## STYLE 02 — Beat-Sync / Fast-Cut Sizzle

**Philosophy:** Energy, speed, and hunger stacked in rhythmic cuts. Food keeps pace with music.
**Emotion:** Excitement, impulse, FOMO.

**Shot structure (15s, 8–12 shots):**
1. Hard hook — ECU macro action (0–1s)
2. Energy intro — dish reveal (1–2.5s)
3. Ingredient beat — 2–3 rapid cuts (2.5–4.5s)
4. Action beats — process, pour, grab (4.5–9.5s)
5. Money shot — slow-mo peak action (9.5–12s)
6. Hero resolve + brand sting (12–15s)

**Camera:** No move longer than 2s. Alternate scale aggressively (ECU → medium → ECU). Whip pans and match cuts. Speed ramps on beat drops. Vertical: favor vertical moves, avoid horizontal whip pans.
**Audio:** Music drives edit, BPM 100–130. Foley synced to cut points. Bass/drum on key food moments.
**Best foods:** Fries, burgers, wings, tacos, pizza slices, fried chicken, combo meals, fast-casual bowls.
**Avoid when:** Fine dining, subtle textures requiring sustained attention, premium sushi.
**Continuity:** Relaxed micro-continuity (viewer can't notice at 1s/shot). But lock dish color, plateware, and hero ingredient identity.

## STYLE 03 — ASMR / Sensory Immersion

**Philosophy:** Intimate closeness. The viewer feels they could touch the food.
**Emotion:** Calm pleasure, tactile satisfaction.

**Shot structure (15s, 3–4 shots):**
1. ECU of most textured surface — hold 2–3s
2. Slow tactile action (pour, crack, press) — hold 3–5s
3. Second texture or aftermath — hold 2–3s
4. Composed result — held still

**Camera:** ECU and macro only. Camera barely moves — micro-drifts. Probe lens preferred. Real-time or ultra-slow. Maximum depth compression.
**Audio:** Sound leads — sizzle, snap, pour, crunch. Music minimal or absent. Boost high-frequency for crispness.
**Best foods:** Fried chicken (crunch), bread (tear/crust), chocolate (snap/pour), grilled meats (sizzle), cheese pulls.
**Avoid when:** Foods with no distinctive sound, broad audience (ASMR is polarizing ~20–30%).
**Continuity:** High — macro scrutiny means any surface/moisture/garnish change is immediately visible.

## STYLE 04 — Process / Transformation

**Philosophy:** The journey makes the result meaningful. Showing craft increases perceived value.
**Emotion:** Anticipation, appreciation of skill, trust in quality.

**Shot structure (15s):**
A. Ingredient-first: ingredients (2–3s) → assembly (4–6s) → heat moment (2–3s) → hero (2–3s)
B. Before→after: raw state (1–2s) → cooking (3–4s) → mid-transform (2s) → hero (3–4s)

**Camera:** Mix of overhead (prep), 45° (cooking), low angle (hero result). Hands and tools visible. Real-time for skilled gestures; slow-mo for heat/sizzle/fracture.
**Audio:** Process sounds lead: sizzle, chop, bubbling. Music builds during process, resolves on hero.
**Best foods:** Artisan pizza, burgers (assembly), ramen, tacos, steaks, fried chicken, baked goods, BBQ.
**Avoid when:** Pre-packaged foods, unappealing intermediate states (grey meat before browning).
**Continuity:** Final dish must logically derive from shown ingredients. Irreversible state changes must not undo.

## STYLE 05 — Float / Gravity-Defying

**Philosophy:** Ingredients defy physics to create wonder. The food becomes spectacular.
**Emotion:** Delight, wonder, brand memorability.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Still hero or pack shot (1–2s)
2. Ingredients rise/separate/orbit (3–4s)
3. Peak suspension — max visual complexity (4–5s)
4. Reassembly into final dish (2–3s)

**Camera:** Clean neutral background. Camera holds still — food does the moving. Motion control (Bolt/robotic arm) for precision. Slow-mo for suspension peak.
**Audio:** Light, playful music during float; confident brand beat on landing. Whoosh micro-sounds as ingredients rise.
**Best foods:** Burgers (deconstruction), tacos, salads, pizza (topping separation), QSR combos, packaged snacks.
**Avoid when:** Liquid-heavy dishes, foods that look worse deconstructed, fine dining (too playful).
**Continuity:** Each suspended component must match its counterpart in assembled hero. State consistency across float and landing.

---

## STYLE 06 — Overhead / Flat-Lay

**Philosophy:** Pattern, geometry, and abundance through the bird's-eye view.
**Emotion:** Orderly desire, visual interest, freshness, completeness.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Wide overhead establishing (2–3s)
2. Macro push-in from above to hero element (3–4s)
3. Action from above: sauce pour, topping scatter, hand reaching (3–4s)
4. Return to wide hero composition (2–3s)

**Camera:** 90° overhead strict flat-lay or 75–85° bird's-eye. Overhead rig, no handheld wobble. Slow push-ins from above; no orbits. No lateral moves breaking overhead plane.
**Audio:** Food sounds from above feel intimate. Chopping, placing, pouring sound close and satisfying. Music light — style is visual-dominant.
**Best foods:** Pizza, grain bowls, salads, prep boards, charcuterie spreads, sushi sets, baking flat lays.
**Avoid when:** Tall stacked items (burger, layer cake) — overhead destroys architectural appeal.
**Continuity:** Every component position fully visible from above — any topping/garnish/sauce change immediately readable. Lock top surface composition.

## STYLE 07 — Macro / Texture-Only

**Philosophy:** Never show the full dish. Only surfaces, edges, and micro-details.
**Emotion:** Primal appetite, fascination, implied richness.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Unknown surface — viewer can't identify food (1–2s)
2. Slight pull-back reveals partial identity (2–3s)
3. Tactile action at macro scale — fracture, drip, press (4–5s)
4. Single partial reveal — recognizable element without full dish (2–3s)

**Camera:** Probe/endoscope lens default. Camera travels through/along surface. No full dish composition. Extreme selective focus (~5mm DOF). Movement always along or into surface.
**Audio:** Sound is primary communication — image is abstract, sound anchors identity. Maximum hero food sound. Music secondary or absent.
**Best foods:** Fried chicken skin, bread crust, pizza char, chocolate, cheese surfaces, steak crust.
**Avoid when:** Uninteresting textures at macro (steamed veg, plain tofu), foods requiring dish identity for context.
**Continuity:** Partially suspended — but same surface across shots must maintain consistent moisture, char, sauce position.

## STYLE 08 — Restaurant POV / Lifestyle

**Philosophy:** The viewer inhabits the dining experience. Environment matters as much as food.
**Emotion:** Belonging, aspiration, social desire, FOMO.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Environmental establishing — restaurant ambience (2–3s)
2. Dish arrival or placement (2–3s)
3. Close-up from diner's perspective (2–3s)
4. Human interaction — hand reaching, glass raised, first bite (3–4s)

**Camera:** 45° "diner's view." Handheld or gimbal for alive movement. Shallow focus on food; background bokeh life. Real-time, no slow motion.
**Audio:** Ambient restaurant atmosphere: murmur, cutlery, soft music. Food sounds intimate and close. Laughter, toast sounds.
**Best foods:** Shareables, fine dining plated, cocktails, pasta, steaks, seasonal specials.
**Avoid when:** QSR (incongruent), packaged goods, foods needing macro scrutiny.
**Continuity:** Environmental continuity: background life, ambient light, table setup. Dish must match between environment shot and close-up.

## STYLE 09 — Stop Motion

**Philosophy:** Frame-by-frame handcrafted movement creates magic and charm.
**Emotion:** Delight, playfulness, artisanal charm, shareability.

**Shot structure (15s):**
A. Ingredient build: components appear one by one
B. Recipe journey: process compressed into magical assembly
C. Product reveal: packaging revealed through animated approach

**Camera:** Overhead or 45°. Camera perfectly locked on tripod. Smooth animation requires 24fps; 12fps for intentional "jerky" charm. Subject-to-camera distance fixed.
**Audio:** Playful sound design matching animated movements. Musical tempo synced to animation frame rate.
**Best foods:** Sandwiches/burgers (assembly), pizza (topping layering), salads, baked goods (recipe build).
**Avoid when:** Foods requiring real texture, steam, or gloss display (stop motion can't replicate hot food physics).
**Continuity:** Inherently fixed-timeline — continuity IS the discipline. Each frame shows only planned state change.

## STYLE 10 — Heritage / Origin / Documentary

**Philosophy:** Where the food came from earns the trust that sells it.
**Emotion:** Trust, appreciation, nostalgia, pride of provenance.

**Shot structure (30–60s, condensable to 15s):**
1. Origin environment — farm, kitchen, countryside
2. Ingredient or process at source
3. Human craft — hands at work
4. Bridge from origin to final product
5. Hero product in context

**Camera:** Observational, slightly handheld "documentary feel." Natural light preferred. Wide establishing → tighter details. Cut on action of real work. Prime lenses only, no zoom.
**Audio:** Ambient environment leads: wind, fire, kitchen sounds. Music warm, culturally congruent. Spoken word/narration optional but powerful.
**Best foods:** Olive oil, wine, cheese, artisan bread, premium meat, specialty coffee, craft chocolate.
**Avoid when:** Fast food, highly processed products where origin story is unappealing.
**Continuity:** Final product must visually connect to origin shown. If warm rustic origin, studio-cold hero shot breaks the contract.

## STYLE 11 — UGC / Authentic / Lo-Fi

**Philosophy:** Imperfection signals trust. Real humans, real lighting, real reactions.
**Emotion:** Relatability, trust, curiosity, social proof.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Immediate hook — person reacting to food (1–2s)
2. Product shown from personal POV (3–4s)
3. Taste/reaction moment — genuine (4–5s)
4. Simple brand mention or product visibility (2–3s)

**Camera:** Smartphone-native. Slight digital noise, occasional autofocus hunting. Self-shot or loosely held. No stabilization.
**Audio:** Natural room sound. Authentic reactions. Trending audio or native sound preferred. No -14 LUFS discipline — slight over-levels signal native content.
**Best foods:** Delivery unboxing, restaurant first-bite, recipe results, snack foods, comfort foods.
**Avoid when:** Fine dining, premium packaging, aspirational authority positioning.
**Continuity:** Lowest expectations — audiences expect natural variation. But unboxed product must match packaging.

---

## STYLE 12 — Deconstruction / Assembly / Visual Engineering

**Philosophy:** Showing the architecture of a dish in motion creates fascination and communicates ingredient quality.
**Emotion:** Respect for complexity, transparency, appetite through component beauty.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Final assembled dish (1–2s)
2. Slow-motion layer separation (5–7s)
3. Suspended float state — each layer distinct (2–3s)
4. Reassembly to hero dish (2–3s)

**Camera:** Motion control essential for repeatability. Camera descends through float. Clean neutral background. Phantom high-speed for micro-movements.
**Audio:** Light, spacious during separation. Increasing density during assembly. Satisfying thud on final landing. Music often minimal.
**Best foods:** Burgers (most iconic), sandwiches, tacos, sushi rolls (cross-section), grain bowls, loaded fries.
**Avoid when:** Soups/liquid dishes, homogeneous foods with no layers, fine dining where component relationships tell the story.
**Continuity:** Each component must match real-world counterpart in assembled hero — same size, moisture, cut direction.

---

## STYLE 13 — Live Fire / Reactive Cooking

**Philosophy:** Heat, flame, and immediacy create unmatched appetite authenticity.
**Emotion:** Excitement, primal appetite, chef respect, trust in craft.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Fire/heat source establishing (2–3s)
2. Food contact with heat — sizzle, flame-lick, wok toss (4–5s)
3. Reaction close-up — caramelization, char, glaze set, steam burst (3–4s)
4. Hero dish plated (2–3s)

**Camera:** Slightly lower angle — heat feels dramatic from below. Handheld for action around fire. Cut between wide (spatial context) and tight (reaction detail). Slow-mo at char/flame/toss peak.
**Audio:** Sizzle, hiss, crackle, flame roar — soundscape IS the selling point. High BPM music or percussive rhythm. Wok toss creates natural beat.
**Best foods:** Steak, wok-fired Asian dishes, BBQ, flambe desserts, wood-fired pizza, yakitori, tacos al pastor.
**Avoid when:** Raw/cold foods (sushi, salads, cold desserts).
**Continuity:** Heat state must persist — food shown on fire must arrive at table with evidence of heat (char, gloss, steam). No raw→perfectly cooked without transition.

---

## STYLE 14 — Beverage / Pour / Condensation

**Philosophy:** Liquid is alive. Movement, clarity, and cold sensation communicate refreshment.
**Emotion:** Thirst, refreshment, immediate desire.

**Shot structure (15s):**
1. Condensation close-up on vessel (1–2s)
2. Pour — liquid entering vessel with impact, foam, or splash (4–5s)
3. Settle — liquid finding level, foam subsiding (2–3s)
4. Hero vessel fully composed (2–3s)

**Camera:** Eye-level or slight low angle. Phantom high-speed for pour impact and splash crown. Push-in toward condensation. Rotation rig for 360° label reveal. Slow-mo pour; real-time presentation.
**Audio:** Pour sounds calibrated to density — slow pour = thick, fast pour = thin. Fizz essential for carbonated. Ice clink for cold drinks.
**Best foods/drinks:** Beer, wine, soda, cocktails, spirits, juice, coffee pour-over, cream pours, sauce drizzles.
**Avoid when:** Beverage is secondary to food, liquid behavior would break food continuity.
**Continuity:** Liquid level changes are irreversible. Foam height changes over time. Condensation: once droplets run, they stay run.

## Style Stacking Logic

Some styles combine within 15s when they occupy different sequence functions:

**Valid combinations:**
- Premium Cinematic (hook + payoff) + Process (middle)
- Macro/Texture (hook) + Restaurant POV (payoff) — intimacy to context
- ASMR (hook) + Premium Cinematic (payoff) — sensory teaser to beauty
- Live Fire (process) + Premium Cinematic (payoff) — craft to hero

**Avoid:**
- Float + ASMR — physics spectacle and intimate closeness fight each other
- Heritage/Origin + Beat-Sync — pace and tone incompatible
- UGC/Lo-Fi + Premium Cinematic — production value gap breaks trust
- Overhead/Flat-Lay + Live Fire — static top-down kills fire's energy
