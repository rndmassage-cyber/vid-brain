# Soups, Sauces, Noodles and Liquid Dishes

## Core Thesis
Liquid dishes are sold through heat, depth, viscosity, and the viewer's ability to imagine a spoonful or slurp.

## Physical Identity of the Food
Key traits:
- visible liquid body
- surface activity or richness
- utensil-driven access
- steam or temperature cues
- ingredients suspended within a fluid system

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- The liquid must have a clear viscosity identity.
- Hero ingredients must not drown invisibly.
- Steam should be localized and believable.
- Surface disturbance must match utensil action.
- The noodle or spoon origin point must remain logical.

## Best Shot Types
- backlit steam close-up
- spoon lift with drip or thickness reveal
- noodle pull from the bowl
- sauce pour or glaze coat
- broth surface macro
- final hero bowl with visible topography

## Best Camera Angles
- 45° for appetite access
- macro side angle for dripping and sheen
- occasional overhead for geometry, but not at the expense of depth

## Best Lighting Traits
- backlight for steam
- broad soft reflections for broth richness
- selective harder highlights on droplets, oil rings, or sauce edges

## Best Interactions
- spoon scoop
- noodle lift
- stir and settle
- drizzle
- dip and release
- bowl placement with steam bloom

## What Makes It Look Expensive
- rich reflective surface
- visible heat
- suspended hero ingredients
- elegant drip behavior
- liquid motion that feels heavy enough
- bowl and spoon logic that supports access

## What Makes It Look Cheap
- flat matte broth
- no steam on hot dishes
- every sauce looking equally thick
- noodles emerging from nowhere
- ingredients lost below the liquid line

## What Must Stay Consistent
- bowl identity
- liquid level family
- broth color and richness
- hero ingredient layout
- utensil size and material
- origin point of lifted contents

## Common Failure Modes in Generative Video
- spoon enters one liquid and exits another
- noodle strands fuse into a solid ribbon
- liquid level changes between cuts with no cause
- broth surface loses oil rings or reflection logic
- drips ignore gravity
