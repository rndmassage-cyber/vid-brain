# Desserts, Beverages and Ice

## Core Thesis
Desserts and beverages are sold through polish, temperature cues, indulgent surface behavior, and a sense of reward.

## Physical Identity of the Food
Key traits:
- reflective glaze, cream, syrup, or glass
- strong hot-or-cold cues
- layered interiors or soft break points
- condensation, fizz, foam, or scoop logic when relevant
- high tolerance for beauty, but low tolerance for dead surfaces

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Decide whether the item is best sold as glossy, creamy, fizzy, frozen, or crackable.
- Cold items need convincing condensation or frost logic.
- Hot desserts or beverages need warmth cues.
- Glass and ice must remain clean and rigid.
- Creams, foams, and scoops should hold believable structure.

## Best Shot Types
- glaze roll close-up
- spoon crack or scoop reveal
- syrup pour
- drink condensation macro
- carbonation or ice impact
- final hero dessert or beverage portrait

## Best Camera Angles
- 30° to 45° for plated desserts
- low to mid angle for drinks and layered glasses
- macro for glaze, bubbles, ice, or spoon entry

## Best Lighting Traits
- broad elegant highlights on smooth surfaces
- harder highlights on droplets, bubbles, or sugar crystals
- backlight for translucent drinks, ice, or steam
- controlled reflections on glassware

## Best Interactions
- spoon crack
- scoop lift
- syrup pour
- cherry or garnish placement
- straw or sip setup
- cube drop into liquid
- foam disturbance

## What Makes It Look Expensive
- clean temperature cues
- premium reflections
- layered interior reveal
- stable cream or foam shape
- believable ice geometry and condensation
- restrained garnish

## What Makes It Look Cheap
- cloudy glass
- melted ice with no logic
- syrup that looks watery or muddy
- dessert surfaces with no sheen or texture contrast
- cream collapsing into shapeless paste

## What Must Stay Consistent
- glass or plate identity
- condensation family
- scoop shape after interaction
- syrup or sauce amount
- garnish placement
- foam height or layer order

## Common Failure Modes in Generative Video
- ice turns into plastic or jelly
- condensation disappears between cuts
- glass reflections change erratically
- cake or dessert layers drift out of alignment
- cream loses structure from shot to shot
