# Sushi, Raw Seafood and Cold Fresh Foods

## Core Thesis
Cold fresh foods are sold through precision, clarity, and clean freshness rather than explosive force.

## Physical Identity of the Food
Key traits:
- exact cuts
- clean geometry
- controlled shine
- translucency in key ingredients
- minimal but disciplined garnish
- high surface cleanliness

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Preserve geometric precision.
- Keep rice grains legible where visible.
- Show fish or fresh components as moist, not oily or heavy.
- Use restraint in motion; these foods do not benefit from chaotic handling.
- Tools such as chopsticks must remain elegant and rigid.

## Best Shot Types
- macro translucency reveal
- glaze or soy dip detail
- chopstick lift
- clean cut-face beauty shot
- cold-condensation or fresh-surface detail
- minimal hero plating payoff

## Best Camera Angles
- 30° to 45° for edible access
- macro side angle for fish edge glow
- overhead only when composition and geometry are the main attraction

## Best Lighting Traits
- strong back or side-backlight for edge glow and translucency
- clean speculars on fish surface
- restrained contrast so precision stays readable

## Best Interactions
- chopstick pinch and lift
- soy dip
- knife reveal for sashimi-style slices
- garnish placement
- subtle plate turn or reveal

## What Makes It Look Expensive
- precise rice or cut geometry
- clean moisture logic
- elegant minimal set
- micro-gloss on fish or glaze
- confident empty space around the dish
- visible freshness without clutter

## What Makes It Look Cheap
- rice merging into a white block
- fish appearing opaque and dead
- too much sauce or garnish
- chaotic motion
- chopsticks clipping or melting into the food

## What Must Stay Consistent
- cut-face geometry
- glaze amount
- garnish placement
- vessel identity
- chopstick material and proportion
- number and order of visible pieces

## Common Failure Modes in Generative Video
- rice grains disappear or fuse
- nori texture becomes rubbery or glossy
- the fish loses translucency
- pieces change count or shape across cuts
- soy sauce behaves like syrup
