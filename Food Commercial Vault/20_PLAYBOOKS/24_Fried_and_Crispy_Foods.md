# Fried and Crispy Foods

## Core Thesis
Crispy foods are sold through fracture, rough topography, and the promise of sound.

## Physical Identity of the Food
Key traits:
- jagged or craggy exterior
- strong micro-contrast
- dry-looking structure with selective shine
- interior contrast when opened
- visible crumbs or flakes during interaction

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Keep the crust topography readable.
- Do not smooth the exterior into a generic shell.
- Show selective gloss, not uniform oil.
- Contrast the crust with a moist interior if the item is opened.
- Use action that creates debris or fracture when possible.

## Best Shot Types
- crust macro
- pull-apart reveal
- bite or break moment
- sauce dip or glaze coat
- crumb explosion close-up
- final hero with crunch cues intact

## Best Camera Angles
- macro side or slight 45° for topography
- low to mid angle for layered crunchy foods
- overhead only if the pattern is the hero

## Best Lighting Traits
- hard or semi-hard directional light for peaks and valleys
- selective specular points on crust ridges
- negative fill to deepen the rugged surface

## Best Interactions
- crack
- break
- pull-apart
- bite reveal
- sauce dip
- seasoning rain
- basket or tray drop with crisp bounce logic

## What Makes It Look Expensive
- a violent but controlled crust landscape
- visible texture hierarchy
- clean contrast between shell and interior
- disciplined sauce or glaze application
- strong debris behavior during action

## What Makes It Look Cheap
- smooth fried coating
- greasy monotone shine
- no visible fracture
- soft-looking crust with no contrast
- interior that looks dry or artificial

## What Must Stay Consistent
- crag pattern family
- sauce coverage
- basket, board, or tray identity
- bone or interior reveal geometry if opened
- crumb aftermath after fracture

## Common Failure Modes in Generative Video
- the crust changes geography between cuts
- flakes disappear after impact
- the interior and exterior blend together
- sauce turns the crust into a flat mass
- bones or rigid elements warp unnaturally
