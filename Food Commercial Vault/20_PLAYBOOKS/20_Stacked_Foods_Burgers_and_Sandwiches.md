# Stacked Foods, Burgers and Sandwiches

## Core Thesis
Stacked foods are sold as edible architecture.  
The viewer must understand layer order, height, softness, and pressure response instantly.

## Physical Identity of the Food
Key traits:
- vertical layering
- visible separation between components
- soft structural elements around firmer cores
- a clear first bite path
- compression without total collapse

## Non-Negotiable Rules
- Lock the exact bottom-to-top layer sequence.
- Keep buns or bread believable, not perfectly geometric.
- Show enough height to feel indulgent, but not so much that eating seems impossible.
- Preserve ingredient readability at the hero angle.
- Compression must release juices or sauce logically.
- Sesame, crumbs, and garnish should remain plausible across shots.

## Best Shot Types
- low hero reveal
- gloved or clean-hand compression
- crown placement or top layer landing
- cross-section cut
- close-up of sauce breach at the edge
- final hero with a clean bite path

## Best Camera Angles
- 0° to 15° for monumentality
- 30° to 45° for readable bite access
- macro side close-up for cheese edge, sauce, or meat texture

## Best Lighting Traits
- back/side-backlight for bun contour and meat gloss
- controlled speculars on juicy components
- negative fill to deepen creases and patty texture

## Best Interactions
- bun press
- layer reveal
- bite setup
- cheese melt edge reveal
- fry lift or drop as side support
- wrapper or serving paper pull-away

## What Makes It Look Expensive
- clean layer definition
- elastic bun compression
- controlled sauce release
- asymmetrical but intentional stack
- premium vessel or tray logic
- visible meat texture and moisture without greasy chaos

## What Makes It Look Cheap
- layers merging into one mass
- perfectly rigid bread
- cheese melting into a flat blanket
- no visible pressure response
- garnish randomly duplicated or missing
- impossible tower with no bite logic

## What Must Stay Consistent
- exact layer order
- bun shape and seed pattern logic
- sauce placement family
- vessel, tray, board, or wrapper
- degree of compression damage if it already happened

## Common Failure Modes in Generative Video
- cheese jumps below the patty
- bun becomes too smooth or plastic
- the stack changes height across shots
- bite marks disappear
- fries or sides warp or soften unnaturally
- wrapper folds change randomly
