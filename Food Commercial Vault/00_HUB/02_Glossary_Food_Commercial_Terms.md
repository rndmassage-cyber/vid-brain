# Glossary of Food Commercial Terms

## Camera Terms
- **Hero angle** — the most flattering angle for the dish, usually the angle that makes it feel fullest, tallest, or most edible.
- **Push-in** — a move that travels toward the food to intensify focus and scale.
- **Orbit** — a curved move around the dish, usually used to show shape, gloss, and depth.
- **Probe-like macro** — an extremely close wide-angle perspective that moves through or near the food and exaggerates scale.
- **Compression** — a tighter lens look that flattens depth and isolates a detail against soft blur.
- **Speed ramp** — an intentional change in playback speed within a shot.

## Lighting Terms
- **Backlight** — light coming from behind the dish, often used for steam, translucency, and edge definition.
- **Edge light** — a rim or highlight along the contour that separates the food from the background.
- **Negative fill** — dark material used to deepen shadow and increase shape contrast.
- **Specular highlight** — a bright reflection that makes food feel wet, juicy, glossy, or fresh.
- **Gloss control** — the decision to widen, soften, sharpen, or suppress reflections depending on the surface.

## Styling Terms
- **Hero dish** — the final version of the dish that appears on camera as the main selling image.
- **Controlled imperfection** — a deliberate touch of natural irregularity that keeps the dish from feeling artificial.
- **Ready-to-eat** — a visual state where the food appears immediately desirable and accessible, not raw, awkward, or unfinished.
- **Garnish map** — the planned position and amount of garnish that should remain consistent across shots.
- **Plateware** — the bowl, plate, tray, board, cup, or vessel containing the food.

## Physics and Texture Terms
- **Compression action** — physical pressure that makes a food yield slightly and release juices or sauce.
- **Tensile action** — pulling forces that create stretch, strands, or resistance.
- **Surface tension** — the visible way liquids hold shape before breaking.
- **Micro-contrast** — tiny highlight/shadow differences that make crusts and textures feel crisp.
- **Aftermath** — the visible result of an action, such as crumbs, sauce displacement, or a lifted slice gap.

## Editing Terms
- **Hook** — the first visual event that grabs attention.
- **Payoff** — the final reward shot where the dish is seen in its most desirable state.
- **Match cut** — a cut that preserves shape, direction, or motion between two shots.
- **Loop return** — an ending beat that can flow back into the opening for seamless replay.
- **Shot function** — the job of the shot: hook, reveal, process, interaction, or payoff.

## Continuity Terms
- **Dish identity lock** — the rule that the dish must remain recognizably the same object throughout the sequence.
- **Layer order lock** — fixed top-to-bottom or bottom-to-top arrangement of stacked components.
- **Prop lock** — plateware, tools, and table surface must not change unless deliberately motivated.
- **Irreversible state change** — an action that cannot logically undo itself, such as slicing, biting, pouring, or removing a piece.
- **State log** — a shot-by-shot record of visual changes.

## Reading Rule
Whenever a term can be described either technically or visually, prefer the visual meaning. The vault is optimized for decision-making in image generation, not for teaching gear operation.
